Investment Landscape
Financial Goals & Savings vs Investment
  • Financial Objective = identifying a life event (education, retirement, house) that needs money. Add amount + timeline → it becomes a Financial Goal.
  • Saving = safety of money first (no risk). Investing = primary aim is profit/growth. Saving precedes investing.
  • Inflation = general rise in prices. It erodes purchasing power. Long-term goals must be adjusted for inflation. E.g. Rs 50 lakh today @ 8% inflation = Rs 1.08 crore in 10 years.
  • Key factors to evaluate investments: Safety, Liquidity, Returns (primary). Also: Convenience, Ticket size, Taxability, Tax deduction.
  • Nominal rate = return without adjusting for inflation. Real rate = return after adjusting for inflation.
  • If real rate is negative → purchasing power is declining even though you earn interest.
  • Pool approach = one pool for all goals — bad practice since you don't know the investment horizon for each goal.

5 Types of Investment Risk
  • Inflation Risk — purchasing power erodes if return < inflation. @ 8% inflation: Rs 10,000 → needs Rs 46,610 after 20 yrs to buy same goods.
  • Liquidity Risk — cannot convert to cash easily (real estate, lock-in FDs, close-ended funds). Penalty for early exit reduces return.
  • Credit Risk — borrower delays or defaults. Govt bonds = lowest credit risk → lowest rate. Corporate bonds = higher risk → higher rate.
  • Market Risk (Systematic) — affects ALL securities (war, recession). Cannot be reduced by diversification.
  • Price / Specific Risk (Unsystematic) — affects ONE company/sector. Can be reduced by diversification.
  • Interest Rate Risk — when rates rise, bond prices fall (inverse). Longer maturity = higher price fluctuation. Affects debt more than equity.
Risk Management Strategies
  • Avoid — don't invest in what you don't understand.
  • Take position — anticipate events (e.g. expect rate cut → buy long-maturity bonds). Requires superior knowledge. Not for all investors.
  • Diversify — spread across unrelated securities. Reduces unsystematic risk. Best strategy for most investors.
  • Credit risk measured by: credit ratings and credit spreads. Volatility risk measured by: variance, standard deviation, beta, modified duration.
Behavioural Biases (8 Key)
  • Availability Heuristic — relying on easily recalled examples; miss critical info.
  • Confirmation Bias — seek info that confirms existing view; ignore contradictory data.
  • Familiarity Bias — prefer known investments; reduces diversification.
  • Herd Mentality — follow the crowd; often leads to buy-high/sell-low.
  • Loss Aversion — fear of losing > desire to gain (Kahneman & Tversky, Nobel Prize). Avoid profitable opportunities.
  • Overconfidence — overestimate ability; take on unassessed risks.
  • Recency Bias — recent events dominate decisions; extrapolate bull/bear markets.
  • Concentration Risk — investing in what you work in (e.g. IT professional only buying tech stocks).
  • Antidote: take help of a Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) or Mutual Fund Distributor (MFD).
Risk Profiling & Asset Allocation
  • Risk profiling evaluates 3 things: Need to take risk (goal requires higher return?), Ability (financial + time horizon), Willingness (psychological capacity).
  • Strategic Asset Allocation — set target % across asset classes aligned to goals. Maintain through rebalancing.
  • Tactical Asset Allocation — dynamically change allocation to capture opportunities. Also called Dynamic Asset Allocation. Suitable for seasoned investors with large surplus.
  • Rebalancing — restore target allocation when deviation occurs (auto buy-low, sell-high). Required for both strategies.
  • SEBI mandated rebalancing period for all schemes (except Index Funds, ETFs, Overnight Funds): 30 business days for passive breaches.

Concept and Role of a Mutual Fund
Core Concepts
  • MF = a Trust that pools money from investors and invests in securities as per stated objectives. Investor invests through MF, not in MF.
  • Unit = investor's share of the scheme. Face Value = typically Rs 10 per unit.
  • NAV = Net Asset Value = true worth per unit = (Total Assets – Liabilities) ÷ Units outstanding.
  • AUM = Assets Under Management = NAV × total units outstanding = total fund size.
  • Mark to Market (MTM) = valuing portfolio at current market price daily → causes daily NAV fluctuation.
  • IDCW = Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal (formerly called Dividend option).
  • MF industry AUM: Rs 11.89 lakh crore (Mar 2015) → Rs 66.70 lakh crore (Mar 2025), growth ~18.82% CAGR.
  • Folios: 4.80 crore (Mar 2010) → 23.45 crore (Mar 2025).
  • SIP contribution: Rs 8,055 cr (Mar 2019) → Rs 26,400 cr (Jan 2025).
Advantages of Mutual Funds
  • Professional Management — expert analysts, fund managers, dealers handle investing.
  • Affordable Diversification — even Rs 500 investment gives exposure to a diversified portfolio.
  • Economies of Scale — large pooled corpus → lower transaction costs, better broker terms.
  • Transparency — SID, SAI, KIM + daily NAV + portfolio disclosure.
  • Liquidity — open-ended schemes allow redemption at any time at NAV.
  • Tax Deferral — MF income exempt; investor tax triggered only on redemption (growth option).
  • Tax Benefit — ELSS gives 80C deduction up to Rs 1.5 lakh (old regime).
  • Systematic Approach — SIP, SWP, STP for disciplined investing.
  • Regulatory Comfort — strictly regulated by SEBI.
Limitations of Mutual Funds
  • No Portfolio Customization — investor cannot pick individual securities (unlike PMS).
  • Choice Overload — thousands of schemes. SEBI categorization (2017) helps reduce this.
  • No Control Over Costs — TER is shared by all; SEBI sets limits.
  • No Guaranteed Returns — MF is a pass-through vehicle. Returns depend on market.
Classification by Structure
  • Open-Ended — no fixed maturity; continuous buy/sell at NAV. Unit capital changes daily.
  • Close-Ended — fixed maturity; sold only during NFO; compulsorily listed on stock exchange post NFO. Typically trades at discount to NAV.
  • Interval Funds — mostly close-ended but opens for transactions at pre-specified intervals. Transaction period: min 2 days. Interval period: max 15 days. Must be listed for liquidity between intervals.
  • ETF (Exchange Traded Fund) — trades like a stock on exchange throughout the day; tracks index; passive; requires demat account; multiple intraday prices.
  • Passive Funds (Index Funds) — portfolio = index composition; low cost; no fund manager discretion.
  • Actively Managed — fund manager picks securities within stated objective; higher expense.

Other Fund Types
  • Fixed Maturity Plans (FMPs) — close-ended debt; portfolio duration ≈ scheme maturity; no post-NFO investment; provides return clarity.
  • Target Maturity Funds (TMF) — mature on specific date; invest in bonds of matching maturity; listed on exchange for exit. Low reinvestment risk.
  • Capital Protection Oriented Funds — close-ended hybrid; most of corpus in debt to protect capital; small portion in equity derivatives for upside.
  • Infrastructure Debt Fund (IDF) — invests 90%+ in debt of infra companies; close-ended; can be MF (regulated by SEBI) or NBFC.
  • Real Estate MF — 35% physical assets; 75% in real estate; valued every 90 days by 2 accredited valuers; close-ended; listed.
  • ESG Funds — Thematic equity sub-category. Strategies: Exclusion, Integration, Best-in-class, Impact, Sustainable Objectives, Transition.
  • Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) — NEW category bridging MF and PMS. Min investment Rs 10 lakh. Allows long-short strategies. Eligible for distributors who meet additional criteria.
  • MF Lite — introduced Dec 2024; framework for passively managed schemes only (index funds, ETFs, FoFs).

Legal Structure of Mutual Funds in India
Structure in One Flow
Sponsor → creates Trust → appoints Trustees → Trustees appoint AMC → AMC manages schemes → Custodian holds assets → RTA maintains investor records
  • MF is constituted as a Trust under Indian Trusts Act, 1882.
  • Governed by: SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996 (as amended).
  • Investors = beneficiaries of the trust.
  • Governed by a Trust Deed executed between sponsors and trustees.
Sponsors
  • Sponsors apply to SEBI for registration and invest in AMC capital.
  • Eligibility: 5 years in financial services; positive net worth in all preceding 5 years; net profit after tax each of the 5 years; average net profit ≥ Rs 10 crore; must be fit and proper person.
  • Must maintain a positive liquid net worth > AMC capital contribution.
Trustees
  • Minimum 4 trustees (or 4 directors on trustee company board).
  • At least 2/3rd must be independent (not associated with sponsor).
  • Cannot be: morally turpitudinous person, economic offence convict, any person connected with AMC, trustee of any other MF.
  • Prior SEBI approval required before appointment.
  • Key duties: enter Investment Management Agreement with AMC; periodically review service contracts; ensure no change in fundamental attributes without proper process; file half-yearly reports to SEBI; review investor complaints; exercise core responsibilities independently.
  • AMC can be terminated by: majority of trustees or 75% of unitholders.
AMC (Asset Management Company)
  • Handles day-to-day management of schemes.
  • Must have net worth ≥ Rs 50 crore (effective Jan 1, 2024).
  • At least 50% independent directors on AMC board.
  • Directors and key personnel must not have moral turpitude or securities law convictions.
  • Change in AMC control requires prior approval of Trustees + SEBI. Unitholders get 30 calendar days to exit at NAV without exit load.
  • Departments: Compliance, Fund Management (analysts → fund managers → dealers), Operations/Customer Service, Sales/Marketing, Finance, HR, IT.
Key Service Providers
Custodian
Holds assets; settles transactions; tracks corporate actions. Registered with SEBI. Appointed by Trustees.
RTA (Registrar & Transfer Agent)
Maintains investor folios; processes buy/sell; generates account statements. Appointed by AMC. Must register with SEBI.
Auditors
Scheme auditor ≠ AMC auditor. Scheme auditor appointed by Trustees.
KRA (KYC Registration Agency)
Central KYC for capital markets. Registered with SEBI. Enables one-time KYC across all intermediaries.
Valuation Agencies
AMFI appointed CRISIL & ICRA to provide valuation matrix for non-traded/thinly traded debt securities.
Depositories
NSDL and CDSL hold MF units in demat form. ETF units compulsorily in demat.
Credit Rating Agencies
Rate debt securities. Critical for Corporate Bond Fund, Credit Risk Fund, Capital Protection Schemes.
Collecting Bankers / Payment Aggregators
Collect investor money. Enable digital payments.
AMFI
  • Association of Mutual Funds in India = body of all registered AMCs.
  • Allots ARN (AMFI Registration Number) to distributors — mandatory to sell MFs.
  • AMFI is NOT a regulator or SRO — it is an industry association.
  • Objectives: maintain ethical standards, investor awareness, represent industry to SEBI/RBI/Govt, regulate distributor conduct (including cancellation of ARN), protect investor interests.
  • Transaction platforms: BSE-StAR MF, NSE NMF-II, MF Utilities (CAN-based aggregation platform).
  • Execution Only Platforms (EOP): Category 1 = register with AMFI (agent of AMCs); Category 2 = register with SEBI as Stock Broker in EOP segment (agent of investor).

Legal and Regulatory Framework
4 Financial Regulators in India
  • RBI — Banking + Money markets + Foreign exchange
  • SEBI — Securities markets (MFs, depositories, RTAs, custodians, credit rating agencies)
  • IRDAI — Insurance
  • PFRDA — Pension markets
  • All come under Ministry of Finance.
Key Investment Restrictions (SEBI MF Regulations)
  • MF must buy/sell securities on delivery basis.
  • MF shall not advance any loans.
  • Investment in listed securities of sponsor group companies: limited to 25% of net assets.
  • Investment in other schemes of same/other MF: limited to 5% of NAV. No management fee on such investments. (Not applicable to FoFs.)
  • No MF shall own more than 10% of a company's paid-up voting capital (or 10% of REIT units of single issuer).
  • Single issuer debt investment limit: 10% of NAV (can extend to 12% with Trustee and AMC board approval). Exceptions: G-Secs, T-bills, Triparty Repo on G-Secs, Index/sector debt ETFs.
  • No unlisted debt instruments (except unlisted NCDs up to 10% of debt portfolio).
  • Parking in short-term deposits of scheduled commercial banks: max 15% of net assets (20% with trustee approval).
  • Open-ended debt funds must maintain min 10% in liquid assets (Cash, G-Secs, T-bills, Repo on G-Secs). Not applicable to liquid and overnight funds.
  • Equity investment per company: max 10% of scheme NAV (except index/sector/industry-specific funds).
  • ELSS: minimum 80% in equity.
  • No investment in unlisted or privately placed securities of sponsor's associate/group companies.
SEBI Advertisement Code — Key Rules
  • Ads must be: accurate, true, fair, clear, complete, unambiguous, concise.
  • No celebrities in scheme-level ads (celebrity endorsements allowed at industry level only — subject to prior SEBI approval + no scheme branding).
  • No false/misleading statements; no testimonials; no rankings based on criteria.
  • Standard warning (mandatory, exact): "Mutual Fund investments are subject to market risks, read all scheme related documents carefully." No addition/deletion of words allowed.
  • In AV ads: standard warning in visual + voice-over at least 5 seconds (14 words).
  • Performance disclosure: CAGR for 1yr, 3yr, 5yr, since inception (if scheme > 3 yrs). Point-to-point return on Rs 10,000 also required.
  • For schemes < 6 months: no past performance disclosure. 6 months to 1 year: simple annualised rate for past 6 months.
  • Fund manager's other schemes: top 3 and bottom 3 if managing >6 schemes.
  • SIFs must have distinct brand name and logo from AMC; may use AMC name for first 5 years. Separate website or dedicated web page required.
Investor Rights
  • Beneficial ownership — proportionate right to scheme assets; can get Unit Certificate or demat holding.
  • Change distributor — written request; no NOC required from existing distributor.
  • Inspect documents — Trust Deed, IMA, Custodial Agreement, RTA Agreement, Memorandum & Articles of AMC.
  • Nominate up to 10 nominees with specified percentage distribution.
  • Pledge units — for loans from banks/NBFCs.
  • Grievance redressal — AMC must redress within 21 calendar days. Status of complaints published in annual report and AMC/AMFI website.
  • Change in fundamental attributes — written communication to all unitholders + newspaper ad (English daily + regional). Unitholders get minimum 30 days to exit at NAV without load.
  • Terminate AMC — 75% of unitholders (by unit holding) can terminate AMC or wind up scheme.
  • Unclaimed amounts — If claimed within 3 years: payment at prevailing NAV. If claimed after 3 years: payment at NAV at end of 3 years.
  • Units freely transferable when held in demat form (except ELSS lock-in period).
Investor Grievance: SCORES
  • SEBI Complaint Redress System (SCORES) — online platform at scores.sebi.gov.in. Lodge, follow up, track complaints. Physical complaints also accepted and scanned into system.
  • SEBI handles complaints against: listed companies/RTAs, brokers/exchanges, DPs/depositories, mutual funds, portfolio managers, KRAs, credit rating agencies, FPIs.
AMFI Code of Conduct for Intermediaries
  • Breach of code: AMFI writes to intermediary → 3 weeks to explain → if unsatisfactory → warning letter → proven 2nd violation → ARN cancelled + all AMCs informed.
  • Intermediary has right of appeal to AMFI.
  • Mis-selling defined as: false/misleading statement; concealing material facts; concealing risk factors; not taking reasonable care to ensure suitability.

Fundamental Attributes of a Scheme
  • Type (open/close/interval; equity/debt/hybrid etc.)
  • Investment Objective (growth/income/both) + asset allocation with min-max ranges
  • Terms of Issue (liquidity provisions, aggregate fees/expenses, safety net/guarantee if any)
  • Any change in fundamental attributes requires: 30-day exit option for unitholders at NAV without load.
Mandatory Ongoing Disclosures
  • Daily NAV — disclosed on AMC website and AMFI website (amfiindia.com) every business day. Liquid/Overnight funds: NAV declared on Sundays/holidays too. Close-ended funds: at least weekly.
  • TER (Total Expense Ratio) — AMC must display daily scheme-wise TER on AMC website and AMFI website in downloadable format. Any change in base TER: 3 working days advance notice to investors via email/SMS and on AMC website. Decrease in TER: communicated immediately.
  • Portfolio Disclosure — monthly (within 10 days of month end) on AMC+AMFI websites. Debt schemes: fortnightly (within 5 days). Half-yearly statements sent by email to registered investors. For SIFs: alternate months (May, Jul, Sep, Nov, Jan, Mar).
  • Financial Results — unaudited half-year results within 1 month of Mar 31 and Sep 30. Published in English daily + regional newspaper.
  • Annual Report — hosted on AMC+AMFI website. Emailed within 4 months of March 31 to registered email investors. Physical copy option for others.
  • Scheme Performance Dashboard — each AMC publishes on website; also available on AMFI website.
Risk-o-meter
  • Pictorial representation of risk to principal. 6 levels:
  • Low (Irish Green) | Low to Moderate (Chartreuse) | Moderate (Neon Yellow) | Moderately High (Caramel) | High (Dark Orange) | Very High (Red)
  • Evaluated monthly. Any change communicated via Notice-cum-Addendum and email/SMS to unitholders.
  • AMC must also disclose risk-o-meter of benchmark when comparing scheme vs benchmark performance.
  • For SIFs: Risk-Band (5 levels, Level 1=Lowest, Level 5=Highest). Evaluated monthly. Disclosed within 10 days of month-end on AMC+AMFI websites. Annual disclosure of changes on March 31.
Non-Mandatory: Fund Factsheet
  • Monthly document published voluntarily by all AMCs. Not a regulatory document but SEBI ad rules apply to it.
  • Contains: AUM, NAV, benchmark, fund manager, SIP returns, sector/security allocation, PE ratio, beta, standard deviation, Sharpe ratio (equity), credit rating profile + duration (debt), loads, expenses, SIP/SWP/STP details.

Fund Distribution & Channel Management
Prerequisites to Become a Distributor
  • Pass NISM Series V-A: Mutual Fund Distributors Certification Examination (mandatory, per SEBI).
  • Exception: Age ≥ 50 or 10+ years experience as of May 31, 2010 → can qualify via CPE program instead of exam.
  • Complete KYD (Know Your Distributor) process: biometric (index finger of right hand at CAMS-PoS) + document verification (PAN + address proof). New ARN applications: online only via AMFI website (from Oct 1, 2024).
  • Register with AMFI → get ARN (AMFI Registration Number). Minimum age 18.
  • Get empaneled with AMCs (armed with ARN).
  • Institutions: employees who sell MFs must have an EUIN (Employee Unique Identification Number) from AMFI. EUIN must be quoted in application forms.
  • New V-B (Foundation) certification available for a limited set of simpler schemes.
Types of Distributors & Distribution Modes
  • Individual agents, banks (MNC, private, PSU, cooperative), NBFCs, national/regional distribution companies, stock brokers, e-commerce/online platforms.
  • Distribution modes: Physical (paper forms at OPOAs), Online (AMC/distributor websites), Stock Exchange platforms (BSE StAR MF, NSE NMF-II), MF Utilities (CAN-based), Mobile apps, New-age platforms (Groww, Kuvera, Paytm Money, Coin etc.).
Revenue: Trail Commission
  • Distributors earn trail commission only (no upfront commission; transaction charges framework withdrawn Aug 2025).
  • Trail commission = % of daily AUM attributable to distributor's clients. Paid monthly or quarterly.
  • Formula: Trail = AUM × commission rate p.a. ÷ 365 (calculated daily).
  • Since AUM = units × NAV, distributor benefits from both new inflows AND market appreciation.
  • Commission payable as long as investor stays invested. No time limit (unlike insurance).
  • No commission on self-business (own investments).
  • AMC pays commission to distributors; NOT deducted from investor contributions.
  • GST on commissions: distributor registered for GST → raises invoice + pays GST. Unregistered distributor → AMC pays under reverse charge mechanism.
  • Additional commission available for B-30 cities (beyond top 30 cities) and for onboarding new/women investors (SEBI circular Nov 2025).
Commission Disclosure & Due Diligence
  • AMCs must disclose on website: total commission paid to distributors meeting any one criterion: >20 locations OR AUM >Rs 100 crore (non-institutional) OR commission >Rs 1 crore p.a. across industry OR >Rs 50 lakh from single AMC.
  • AMFI publishes consolidated data on its website.
  • Excessive portfolio turnover (>2× industry average) → AMC must conduct additional due diligence on that distributor.
Distributor vs Investment Advisor
  • Distributors cannot call themselves Investment Advisors.
  • Investment Advisor: registered with SEBI under IA Regulations 2013; cannot earn distribution commission (activities must be separated).
  • Distributor's advice is incidental to distribution — exempted from IA registration.
  • All distributor-client transactions categorized as either Advisory (must recommend appropriate product) or Execution Only (written confirmation of unsuitability required from investor).
Nomination & Change of Distributor
  • AMCs offer nomination facility to distributors for trail commission income protection in case of death.
  • Nominee/legal heir receives commission until AUM under deceased ARN becomes nil or investor changes distributor code.
  • No new business or new SIP/STP registrations under deceased MFD's ARN.
  • Transfer of AUM to nominee's ARN allowed if nominee is a valid ARN holder (KYD compliant).
  • Change of distributor: investor sends written request; AMC complies without requiring NOC from existing distributor. No commission paid to either old or new distributor (except if investor-initiated due to voluntary cessation → new distributor gets trail).
  • Distributor-initiated AUM transfer allowed only for: change in legal status (individual → firm), merger/acquisition, family transfer, or business transfer. Old ARN must be surrendered.

NAV, Total Expense Ratio and Pricing of Units
NAV Formula & Calculation
NAV = (Total Assets − Liabilities other than to Unitholders) ÷ Number of Outstanding Units
  • Total Assets = Market value of investments + Dividend/interest accrued (not yet received) + Receivables (amount due on shares sold) + Current assets.
  • Liabilities = Expenses payable + Payables for securities bought + Borrowings (max 6 months for liquidity needs).
  • NAV is higher when: interest/dividends earned are higher, portfolio appreciates, expenses are lower.
  • NAV accuracy: 4 decimal places — index funds, liquid funds, other debt funds. At least 2 decimal places — equity and balanced funds.
  • Units held in fractions (<1 unit) are allowed; but exchange trading may restrict to whole units.
Mark to Market
  • Portfolio valued at current market price daily → daily NAV fluctuations.
  • Without MTM: new investors buying at old price would dilute NAV for existing investors (or vice versa). MTM ensures fairness.
  • Valuation: traded securities → last closing price on principal stock exchange. Non-traded (>30 days without trade) → "in good faith" by AMC board-approved methodology.
  • Gold (for Gold ETF): valued at LBMA AM fixing price in USD/troy oz (fineness 995.0).
  • Silver (for Silver ETF): valued at LBMA AM fixing price in USD/troy oz (fineness 999.0).

  • Additionally allowed over TER limits: Brokerage/transaction costs up to 0.12% (cash market) and 0.05% (derivatives).
  • Additional expenses of 0.05% if exit load is applicable to the scheme.
  • GST on investment and advisory fees charged OVER TER limits; GST on all other fees within TER limits.
  • Excess expense over limits → borne by AMC.
  • AMC must disclose TER daily on website and AMFI website in downloadable format.
Entry Load, Exit Load & Stamp Duty
  • Entry Load — BANNED by SEBI. Sale Price = NAV (subject to stamp duty).
  • Exit Load — allowed; deducted from NAV on redemption. Must be credited back to scheme immediately (not retained by AMC). No exit load on bonus units or dividend reinvestment units. No distinction between investors based on subscription amount.
  • Exit load can be calibrated by holding period (e.g. 1% within 1 year, nil after 1 year).
  • Stamp Duty on purchase/SIP/switch-in: 0.005% of investment amount (from July 1, 2020). Units allotted on amount after stamp duty deduction.
  • Stamp Duty on transfer of MF units (between demat accounts): 0.015%.
Dividends & Distributable Reserves
  • Dividend = IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal). Declared only from distributable surplus.
  • Distributable reserves: all profits (accrual basis) are available; but valuation gains are excluded (unrealized); valuation losses must be adjusted. Portion of sale price attributable to valuation gains not available.
  • This conservative approach ensures dividend paid from real realized profits.
  • Trustees decide dividend quantum and record date. For monthly or less frequency: can delegate to AMC officials; ratified in next board meeting.
  • Record date = 5 calendar days from AMC notice. AMC must give public notice within 1 business day of decision.
  • NAV adjusted (falls) after dividend payment. Ex-dividend NAV < Cum-dividend NAV.
  • Interest on delayed dividend payment: 15% p.a. from record date (charged to AMC).
Segregated Portfolio
  • Created when a credit event occurs in a debt MF scheme to protect investors from further loss.
  • Segregated portfolio = distressed/illiquid assets separated from rest of portfolio.
  • AMC cannot charge investment and advisory fees on segregated portfolio. TER (excluding advisory fees) can be charged only upon recovery of investments in segregated portfolio.
  • NAV of segregated portfolio declared daily. Disclosed in all scheme documents.

Taxation
MF Income Exemption
  • Income earned by mutual fund schemes = exempt from tax under Section 10(23)(D) of Income Tax Act (MF constituted as trust for benefit of unitholders).
  • Investor pays tax on income received from MF (capital gains, dividends).
  • Equity-oriented fund (for tax): scheme holding more than 65% in equity shares listed on recognized Indian stock exchanges. FoF investing in equity MFs = non-equity-oriented (still taxed as debt).

  • Only 2 holding periods: 12 months (listed securities + equity-oriented funds) and 24 months (all others).
  • LTCG exemption for equity funds: first Rs 1.25 lakh exempt per year.
  • Grandfathering (2018): For equity MFs, cost basis = higher of (actual cost OR NAV on Jan 31, 2018). Gains till Jan 31, 2018 not taxed.
  • Investments before July 23, 2024 redeemed before that date: old tax rates apply. New rates for redemptions on or after July 23, 2024.
Dividend (IDCW) Taxation
  • Old regime (pre Feb 2020): Dividend Distribution Tax (DDT) deducted from scheme; dividend tax-free in investor's hands.
  • New regime (from Feb 2020): DDT abolished. Dividend added to investor's total income and taxed at applicable slab rate.
  • TDS on dividend: 10% TDS if dividend amount exceeds Rs 5,000 per year (for resident investors).
  • Growth option more tax-efficient: tax deferred until redemption; compounding benefits pre-tax.
  • From April 1, 2021: MF must indicate how much of IDCW is income distribution (NAV appreciation) vs capital distribution — taxed differently.
Securities Transaction Tax (STT)
  • STT applicable on redemption/sale of equity-oriented MF units only (not purchase; not debt funds).
  • Sale/redemption of equity MF on stock exchange (delivery based): 0.001% (seller pays).
  • Sale/redemption of equity MF to the fund: 0.001% (seller pays).
  • Non-delivery based sale on exchange: 0.025%.
  • Purchase of equity MF units: Nil.
Tax Benefit: Section 80C & ELSS
  • ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) qualifies for 80C deduction up to Rs 1.5 lakh per year (old tax regime only).
  • Lock-in: 3 years from date of each investment (each SIP instalment locked for 3 years from its date).
  • In new tax regime: ELSS = just another equity MF with 3-year lock-in (no 80C benefit).
  • Tax benefit goes to first holder in joint accounts.
  • Some retirement-oriented funds also qualify for 80C (5-year lock-in).
Capital Loss Set-off Rules
  • Capital loss CANNOT be set off against salary or other income heads.
  • Short-term capital loss → set off against STCG or LTCG.
  • Long-term capital loss → set off against LTCG only.
  • Bonus stripping: If original units bought within 3 months before bonus record date AND sold within 9 months after → capital loss not available for set-off; treated as cost of acquisition of bonus units.
  • TDS for NRI investors: applicable on redemption at rates per income tax rules or DTAA (whichever is lower).
  • GST: AMC charges GST on investment/advisory fees over and above TER limits. GST on exit load deducted from exit load amount before crediting to scheme.

Investor Services
NFO Process & Key Dates
  • NFO = New Fund Offer (first time a scheme is offered to public).
  • NFO can remain open: minimum 3 working days; maximum 15 days (except ELSS). ELSS may stay open longer.
  • Allotment of units / refund: within 5 business days of closure. If refunded late → interest @ 15% p.a. from closure date (charged to AMC).
  • Open-ended schemes must re-open for sale/repurchase within 5 business days of allotment.
  • NFO price = Rs 10 per unit (face value).
  • Close-ended scheme has NFO Open + Close dates but NO re-opening date (listed on stock exchange instead).
Investment Options: Direct vs Regular Plan
  • Regular Plan = routed through distributor (ARN mentioned). Higher TER (includes distribution cost). Higher trail commission paid.
  • Direct Plan = investor applies directly (mention "Direct" where ARN goes). Lower TER (no distribution expense). Lower NAV → same portfolio but lower deduction. No commission paid.
  • Both plans have separate NAVs because of different TER. Cannot offer any other plan variant.

Account Statements
  • Monthly statement: issued if any transaction during the month.
  • Annual statement: for investors with no transactions in last 6 months. Issued annually.
  • Consolidated Account Statement (CAS): across all schemes of all MFs. Issued monthly within 10 days if transaction occurred; half-yearly (within specified timeline) if no transaction. Common PAN used to identify investor across fund houses.
Eligible Investors
  • Resident Indian adults (singly or jointly, max 3); Minors through guardian; HUFs (Karta signs); NRIs/PIOs (repatriable or non-repatriable); Foreign investors (QFI route via demat or UCR).
  • Non-individuals: Companies, registered societies, co-ops, trusts, partnership firms, banks, other MFs, FPIs, multilateral agencies, army/navy/air force institutions, universities etc.
  • Max joint holders: 3. Tax benefits go to first holder.
  • Folio held jointly: cannot add/delete joint holders (except death).
  • Minor: guardian operates account. On majority → account frozen; guardian replaced by major through MAM form. SIPs continue only till date of majority.
Payment Modes
  • Accepted: Cheque, DD, NEFT/RTGS/IMPS, NACH (replaces ECS), UPI, Debit card, Net banking, ASBA (NFO), e-Wallet (max Rs 50,000 per investor per MF per year).
  • NOT accepted: Cash (except max Rs 50,000 per investor per MF per FY for small investors), Credit card, Postal orders, Money orders, Outstation cheques, Post-dated cheques (except SIP).
  • Third-party payments: generally not allowed. Exception: parents/grandparents for minor (up to Rs 50,000 per transaction/instalment), employer for employee payroll deduction, custodian for FPI.
  • Redemption: Direct credit, NEFT, NACH, RTGS to registered bank account. Cheque if electronic not available.
  • Individual investors: max 5 bank accounts can be registered; non-individual: max 10.
  • Instant Access Facility (IAF): same-day redemption from Liquid/Overnight funds. Limit: Rs 50,000 or 90% of investment value, whichever is lower. Per day per scheme per investor.
KYC Requirements
  • All investors (individual + non-individual, NRIs, guardians, PoA holders, joint holders) must be KYC compliant regardless of investment amount.
  • PAN mandatory for all (except PAN-exempt categories).
  • PAN-exempt cases: Micro-SIPs (annual ≤ Rs 50,000); small investors (lump sum ≤ Rs 50,000 per MF per FY); Sikkim residents; Central/State govt officials; UN entities; investing in states/UTs with specific exemptions.
  • KYC Registration Agencies (KRAs) — central KYC for capital markets. Once done with any SEBI-registered intermediary → valid across all securities market. cKYCR managed by CERSAI (from Aug 1, 2016 for new individual accounts).
  • E-KYC via UIDAI Aadhaar accepted. IPV (In-Person Verification) mandatory.
  • FATCA/CRS: required for investors whose birth/citizenship/nationality/tax residency is outside India. Must report to Indian tax authorities for onward transmission to foreign authorities. Changes reported within 30 days.
Systematic Transactions
  • SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) — fixed amount invested at fixed intervals. Benefit = Rupee Cost Averaging (more units when NAV low, fewer when NAV high). Can be daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly. Min 3-year SIP for ELSS but each instalment locked individually.
  • SIP Top-up — increase SIP amount periodically (fixed amount or percentage). Upper limit or end date can be set.
  • SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) — fixed amount redeemed periodically. Protects from lump-sum exit at market trough. Exit load and taxes apply per instalment.
  • STP (Systematic Transfer Plan) — periodic switch from source scheme to target scheme. = SWP from source + SIP into target. Source scheme exit load applies. Minimum investment limits of target scheme don't apply.
  • Transfer of IDCW plan (DTP) — dividend earned in one scheme transferred to another scheme of same MF instead of payout. Overrides existing payout/reinvestment instruction.
  • Triggers — automatic redemption or switch when pre-defined condition met (NAV level, index level, date, percentage gain/loss). One-time facility (extinguished when triggered). Separate trigger request per investment.
Non-Financial Transactions (Key Rules)
  • Nomination: Up to 10 nominees with percentage split (if no split → equal distribution). Nominee holds units in trust for legal heirs. POA holder cannot nominate. Can be changed anytime (all unit-holders must sign). In demat mode: DP nomination applies.
  • Pledge/Lien of units: all unit-holders must sign pledge form. Pledged units cannot be sold until lender gives NOC. On enforcement → lender redeems directly. Min redemption limits don't apply to lien enforcement.
  • Demat: ETF units compulsorily in demat. Open-ended MF: optional but AMC must offer it. ISIN required per option of scheme. KYC done for demat = KYC done for MF too.
  • Transmission: Joint folio → surviving holder(s) substituted. Single folio with nominee → transmitted to nominee. No nominee → to legal heirs. Death certificate + KYC of claimant required.
  • Units redeemed FIFO basis (First-In-First-Out).
  • Unclaimed amounts deployed in separate plan of Liquid scheme. AMC max fee: 0.50% p.a. No exit loads on this plan.

Risk, Return and Performance of Funds
General Risk Factors in MF Schemes
  • Market/Systematic risk — economy-wide; affects all securities; cannot be diversified away. Measured by beta.
  • Liquidity risk — difficulty in selling securities at fair price (thinly traded stocks, small-cap stocks). Also: redemption risk in debt funds during stress periods.
  • Credit risk — default/downgrade by bond issuer. Higher in lower-rated bonds. Credit rating agencies provide initial input; fund manager does own due diligence.
  • Interest rate risk — bond prices fall when rates rise (inverse). Longer duration = greater sensitivity.
  • Reinvestment risk — coupon received reinvested at lower rate than original.
  • Call/Prepayment risk — issuer repays before maturity; investor must reinvest at lower rates.
  • Inflation risk — purchasing power erosion; hits debt funds hardest.
  • Concentration risk — over-exposure to single issuer/sector.
  • Currency risk — for international funds; exchange rate changes affect returns.
  • Settlement risk — counterparty fails to deliver; more relevant in derivatives.
  • Political/Regulatory risk — policy changes affecting specific industries.
Measures of Return
  • Absolute Return = (Current Value − Cost) ÷ Cost × 100. Useful for < 1 year.
  • Annualised Return (CAGR) = [(Ending value ÷ Beginning value) ^ (1/n) − 1] × 100 where n = years. For >1 year.
  • XIRR — used for SIP returns (multiple cash flows at different dates). Most accurate for SIPs.
  • Total Return = capital gain + dividend income.
  • Trailing Returns — past returns for specific periods (1yr, 3yr, 5yr).
  • Rolling Returns — average of all 1-year (or N-year) returns for a given period. Better than trailing to judge consistency.
  • Point-to-Point Return = return from specific start date to specific end date.
  • SEBI requires MF performance advertised as: CAGR for 1yr, 3yr, 5yr, since inception + point-to-point on Rs 10,000 investment.
  • Benchmark must be Total Return Index (TRI) not just Price Return Index (PRI). TRI includes dividends/interest reinvested.

Credit Risk Provisions
  • Segregated Portfolio: created on credit event in debt scheme. Protects unitholders who were invested at time of event from being disadvantaged by redemptions after the event. AMC cannot charge advisory fees on segregated portfolio.
  • Potential Risk Class (PRC) Matrix: classifies debt schemes by maximum interest rate risk (Macaulay Duration) and maximum credit risk (Credit Risk Value). Introduced Dec 2021.
  • Corporate Debt Market Development Fund (CDMDF): backstop facility to buy investment-grade corporate bonds during market stress. Funded by MF contributions.
  • AT-1 Bonds: deemed maturity = 100 years (for valuation purposes, phased in from Apr 2023). No more than 10% of scheme in AT-1 bonds; max 5% from single issuer. Valued on Yield to Call basis.
Factors Affecting MF Performance
  • Equity funds: Economic factors (GDP, inflation, interest rates, fiscal policy), Industry factors (competitive dynamics, pricing power, regulatory changes), Company factors (management quality, earnings growth, debt levels).
  • Debt funds: Interest rate movements (inversely affect bond prices), Credit quality of portfolio, Liquidity of instruments, Duration of portfolio, Yield curve shape.
  • Expense ratio directly reduces returns: higher TER → lower NAV growth → lower returns.

Mutual Fund Scheme Performance
Benchmarks
  • Price Return Index (PRI): only tracks price changes of constituent stocks (capital gains only).
  • Total Return Index (TRI): tracks price changes AND dividend income (dividends notionally reinvested). SEBI mandates all MF schemes benchmarked against TRI. More appropriate because MF scheme captures both.
  • Scheme must have a primary benchmark AND an additional benchmark (often Sensex or Nifty as additional for equity; 10-yr G-Sec for debt).

Quantitative Performance Measures
  • Alpha: Outperformance vs benchmark adjusted for market risk. Positive alpha = fund manager added value. Negative alpha = underperformed on risk-adjusted basis.
  • Beta: Benchmark sensitivity. Beta >1 = more volatile than benchmark. Passive funds should have beta ≈ 1.
  • Sharpe Ratio: (Fund Return − Risk-free rate) ÷ Standard Deviation. Compare funds with different return AND risk profiles.
  • Treynor Ratio: Like Sharpe but uses beta instead of SD. Better for comparing well-diversified funds.
  • Tracking Error: Standard deviation of difference between fund return and benchmark return. Lower = fund tracks index closely. Critical for index funds and ETFs. Target: as close to zero as possible.
  • SEBI norms: Performance advertised as CAGR for 1yr, 3yr, 5yr, since inception. Must show benchmark TRI comparison. If scheme < 6 months: no past performance. 6-12 months: simple annualised 6-month return.
Scheme Performance Disclosure Rules
  • Fund manager advertising: if >6 schemes managed → disclose top 3 and bottom 3 performers (in addition to scheme being advertised).
  • If fund manager changed during performance period → disclose in footnote.
  • Direct vs Regular plan performance must be clearly specified in advertisement.
  • Scheme dashboard on AMC website + AMFI website updated regularly.

Mutual Fund Scheme Selection
Step 1: Understand Investor's Need
  • Financial goal → investment horizon → risk profile → asset allocation. This determines the category of MF scheme.
  • Short-term goals (< 1 year): Overnight fund, Liquid fund, Ultra-Short Duration fund.
  • Short-to-medium goals (1-3 years): Short Duration fund, Low Duration fund, Money Market fund.
  • Medium goals (3-5 years): Medium Duration fund, Corporate Bond fund, Conservative Hybrid fund.
  • Long-term goals (> 5 years): Equity funds (large cap, multi cap, flexi cap), Aggressive Hybrid fund, ELSS.
  • Regular income: Monthly IDCW options, SWP from debt funds, Dividend Yield funds.
  • Tax saving: ELSS (old regime).
  • Inflation beating: Equity funds (long term), Multi-Asset funds.

Step 2: Select Scheme Within Category
  • Within the right category → compare multiple AMCs' schemes on same category.
  • Use: consistent long-term performance (rolling returns, not just recent), fund manager track record, AMC reputation and stability, TER (lower = better for similar returns), AUM size (very small = risk; very large can hamper performance in small-cap), portfolio quality (credit ratings for debt; PE ratio for equity), tracking error (for index funds).
  • Past performance ≠ guarantee of future returns. But consistency across market cycles matters more than one great year.
  • Use SIP for equity; lump sum for debt or when market is at known low.
Selecting Options Within a Scheme
  • Growth vs IDCW: Growth preferred for most investors (tax deferral, compounding). IDCW payout for those needing regular income.
  • Regular vs Direct: Direct = lower TER = higher long-term returns. Use Direct only if investor does their own research and monitoring. Otherwise, use Regular through MFD for guidance.
  • SIP vs Lump-sum: SIP for equity (rupee cost averaging); Lump-sum for debt (predictable returns) or when market is significantly down.
Key Do's and Don'ts
  • ✅ DO: Match scheme to goal, risk profile, horizon. Diversify across 3-5 schemes (not too many). Review portfolio periodically (not daily). Use SIP for volatility management. Read scheme documents before investing.
  • ❌ DON'T: Chase past performers (recency bias). Switch too frequently (taxes + exit loads). Invest based only on AUM size or star rating. Select schemes only because of distributor commission. Treat MF as FD substitute without understanding risk. Select too many schemes (over-diversification loses benefit).
  • Mis-selling = recommending unsuitable scheme; concealing risks; making false claims; not taking care to ensure suitability. Distributor is personally liable for mis-selling.
  • Churning (excessive portfolio turnover by distributor) = not in investor's interest. SEBI monitors distributor portfolio turnover (>2× industry average triggers due diligence).
Investment Strategy Framework by AMC
  • Growth style: invest in companies with high growth potential, even at premium valuation. Higher volatility; better for long-term.
  • Value style: invest in undervalued companies. May underperform in bull markets but more resilient.
  • Blend/Core style: mix of growth and value.
  • Focused fund: max 30 stocks; concentrated bets; higher risk per stock; higher potential reward.
  • Diversified fund: 50-100+ stocks; risk spread; better for risk-averse investors.
  • For debt: Active duration management (fund manager calls on rate direction) vs Fixed maturity (stable, predictable). Credit quality: high quality (lower risk/return) vs credit risk (higher risk/return).
